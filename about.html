About Hi, I'm Desderio

Building Bridges Between Technology & Social Impact

From the challenges of Northern Uganda to the forefront of tech innovation, my journey has been fueled by resilience and a commitment to creating solutions that empower communities. As a software engineer, entrepreneur, and advocate, I bridge the digital divide through sustainable technology and grassroots initiatives.

How I Got Here

Born in Lira City to a Protestant mother who owned a local salon, I grew up witnessing the intersection of poverty, education gaps, and mental health challenges in post-conflict Northern Uganda. My childhood—marked by financial hardship but fueled by relentless curiosity—forged my twin passions: technology as a tool for change and community empowerment as a lifelong mission. Despite the obstacles, I pursued education with determination, first in Electrical Installation and Maintenance, then pivoting to computer science where I discovered my calling at the intersection of code and social impact.

My Values & Beliefs

Sustainable Innovation

Technology should solve real problems without creating new ones. I build solutions that are environmentally conscious, economically viable, and socially inclusive—designed to last and adapt to future needs.

Community First

Lasting change comes from empowering people, not just deploying technology. My work with For-Us Foundation and African Youth Congress reflects this belief—creating tools and programs that enable communities to shape their own futures.

Radical Collaboration

The complex challenges we face require multidisciplinary approaches. I actively seek partnerships across sectors—from tech companies to NGOs—because transformative solutions emerge at these intersections.

Lifelong Learning

In our rapidly evolving digital landscape, staying curious is non-negotiable. I dedicate time weekly to exploring emerging technologies, from quantum computing to AI ethics, ensuring my solutions leverage cutting-edge yet responsible innovation.

Why Work With Me

What sets my approach apart is the fusion of technical expertise with deep community understanding. As Senior Software Developer at Kakebe Technologies, I've engineered solutions used by 300K+ users while maintaining a commitment to accessibility. My SEO strategies have boosted client visibility by 40-200%, and my leadership at For-Us Foundation has created tangible social impact through technology. This dual perspective allows me to build systems that are both technically robust and genuinely user-centered.

Whether developing the For-Us Mental Health App to bridge Uganda's mental health resource gap or advocating for youth employment policies as President of the African Youth Congress, I measure success by real-world impact. My work proves that sustainable development starts with local innovators who understand both the technology and the cultural context.

A Few More Words About Myself

Beyond code and committees, you'll find me volunteering with Journey International to support families affected by Nodding Syndrome, or designing low-bandwidth educational apps for offline refugee settlements. My vision includes establishing a tech hub in Northern Uganda to nurture the next generation of innovators, and scaling For-Us Foundation into an internationally recognized organization. I believe the future belongs to those who can align technological advancement with human dignity—this belief shapes every project I undertake.
